# Getting started

Welcome to BookDB — a personal catalogue for your book collection. This guide walks you through the first things you'll want to do.

## Add your first book

The fastest way to add a book is by its ISBN:

1. Press **Ctrl+N**, or use **File → New book**, or the toolbar's add button.
2. Type or scan the **ISBN** and press **Enter**.
3. BookDB looks the book up across several public sources and opens the **Merge review** dialog, where you pick the title, authors, cover, and other details to keep.
4. Choose a **collection** for the book and click **Save** — or **Save & open editor** to fill in more.

Don't have an ISBN? Choose **Enter details manually** in the same dialog and type the title, authors, and any other fields yourself.

## Import an existing collection

Already have a library in Readerware? You don't have to re-enter it:

- Use **File → Import Readerware backup…**, or the **Import Readerware backup** button on the empty-library screen.
- Point the wizard at a Readerware **backup .zip** or the extracted **backup folder**.
- The wizard previews what it found, lets you pick a target collection, then imports.

See the **Import guide** tab for a step-by-step walkthrough.

## Connect to a database

By default, BookDB keeps your library in a local file on this computer — nothing else to set up.

To share one library across devices, or to use PostgreSQL / MySQL, open **Settings → Database** (or the **Connect to a database** button on the empty-library screen) and enter the connection details. The **Remote databases** tab explains the options.

## Organise your library

- **Collections** group your books (Fiction, Non-Fiction, Comics, …). Set one when you add a book, and change it any time by editing the book.
- **Edit** a book to adjust its details, cover, and contributors. Author and other contributor names reuse existing people as you type, so the same person isn't duplicated.
- **Search and filter** from the toolbar to find anything as your library grows.
